Physical Recovery

Healing After Birth

Your body needs time to heal after giving birth. Whether you had a vaginal delivery or cesarean section, it's important to take care of yourself. Rest when you can, accept help from others, and don't hesitate to contact your healthcare provider with any concerns.

Postpartum Exercise

Gentle movement like short walks can help with recovery. Start slow and gradually increase activity as your body allows. Always get clearance from your healthcare provider before beginning any exercise program after childbirth.

Nutrition Tips

Eating well supports your recovery and, if breastfeeding, your milk production. Focus on nutrient-dense foods, stay hydrated, and consider continuing your prenatal vitamins as recommended by your healthcare provider.

Mental Wellbeing

Baby Blues vs. Postpartum Depression

It's normal to experience mood swings, anxiety, or sadness after having a baby. Baby blues typically resolve within two weeks. If symptoms persist or worsen, it may be postpartum depression, which requires professional support. Remember, seeking help is a sign of strength.

Self-Care Strategies

Even small moments of self-care can make a difference. Try to sleep when your baby sleeps, ask for help with household tasks and baby care, connect with other parents, and take short breaks to do things you enjoy.

When to Seek Help

Contact your healthcare provider if you experience persistent feelings of sadness or emptiness, loss of interest in activities you used to enjoy, thoughts of harming yourself or your baby, or difficulty bonding with your baby.

Baby Care

Feeding Your Baby

Whether you choose to breastfeed, formula feed, or a combination, fed is best. Newborns typically need to eat every 2-3 hours. Watch for hunger cues like rooting, sucking motions, or hand-to-mouth movements.

Sleep Safety

Always place your baby on their back to sleep on a firm, flat surface without pillows, blankets, or toys. Room-sharing (but not bed-sharing) is recommended for the first 6-12 months to reduce the risk of SIDS.

Newborn Care Basics

Regular diaper changes, gentle bathing 2-3 times per week, umbilical cord care, and dressing your baby appropriately for the temperature are important aspects of newborn care. Talk to your pediatrician about any specific concerns.
