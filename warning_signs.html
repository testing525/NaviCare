Warning Signs of Hemorrhage
Mga senyales ng babala ng pagdurugo

For more information, visit Urgent Warning Signs

Mga senyales ng babala ng pagdurugo

Kahulugan: Ang PPH ay labis na pagdurugo pagkatapos ng panganganak, na karaniwang tinutukoy bilang pagkawala ng higit sa 500 mL pagkatapos ng panganganak sa vaginal o 1,000 mL pagkatapos ng cesarean section. Maaari itong mangyari sa loob ng 24 na oras (pangunahin) o hanggang 12 linggo pagkatapos ng panganganak (pangalawang)

Mga Palatandaan ng Babala:

  • Malakas na pagdurugo, pagbababad ng higit sa isang pad bawat oras

  • Pagpasa ng malalaking namuong dugo

  • Pagkahilo, pagkahilo, o panghihina

  • Mabilis na tibok ng puso

  • Malabo ang paningin

  • Pagduduwal o pagsusuka

  • Pamamaga at pananakit sa vaginal o perineal area

Tugon:

  • Humingi kaagad ng emerhensiyang pangangalagang medikal.

  • Habang naghihintay ng tulong, humiga at itaas ang iyong mga binti upang mapabuti ang daloy ng dugo.

  • Huwag subukang magmaneho sa iyong sarili; tumawag sa mga serbisyong pang-emergency.

Warning Signs of Infection

Postpartum infections can occur in the uterus, urinary tract, or at incision sites. Watch for these warning signs:

Infection Warning Signs Illustration

• Fever of 100.4°F (38°C) or higher

• Chills or feeling unusually hot

• Severe abdominal pain or tenderness

• Foul-smelling vaginal discharge

• Pain, redness, or discharge from a C-section incision

• Burning sensation when urinating

• Breast pain, redness, or warmth (signs of mastitis)

Early treatment of infections is important. Contact your healthcare provider if you notice any of these symptoms.

Postpartum Anxiety and Depression

Postpartum mood disorders are common and treatable. Be aware of these symptoms:

Mental Health Warning Signs Illustration

• Feeling sad, hopeless, or overwhelmed for more than two weeks

• Loss of interest in activities you used to enjoy

• Difficulty bonding with your baby

• Changes in appetite or sleep patterns

• Excessive worry or racing thoughts

• Panic attacks or constant anxiety

• Thoughts of harming yourself or your baby

If you're experiencing any of these symptoms, reach out to your healthcare provider. Postpartum mood disorders are not a sign of weakness and can be effectively treated with proper support.
